Life Skill

Why Budgeting Matters

A budget is a plan for your money. It tells every peso where to go BEFORE you spend it.

Benefits

• Avoid overspending
• Track where money goes
• Save for goals (gadget, travel, emergency)
• Reduce financial stress
• Make informed decisions
• Professional skill for AIS careers

Income − Expenses = Savings (or Deficit)

The 50-30-20 Rule

50% = Needs (food, rent, transport, utilities)
30% = Wants (entertainment, shopping, dining out)
20% = Savings/Debt repayment

Example: ₱5,000 monthly allowance
Needs: ₱2,500 | Wants: ₱1,500 | Save: ₱1,000

Structure

Budget Categories

Income Sources

  • 💰 Allowance from parents
  • 💼 Part-time job / freelance
  • 🎁 Gifts / monetary rewards
  • 📈 Scholarship stipend

Fixed Expenses

  • 🏠 Rent / boarding
  • 🚌 Transportation (regular fare)
  • 📱 Phone load / WiFi
  • 📚 School fees / materials

Variable Expenses

  • 🍔 Food (can vary daily)
  • 🎬 Entertainment
  • 🛒 Personal shopping
  • 🏥 Health / medicine

Savings Goals

  • 🎯 Emergency fund
  • 💻 Gadget fund
  • ✈️ Travel fund
  • 🎓 Future investment

Unit 3 Integration: Budget Scenario + Presentation

Performance Task

Your Scenario

Group task (3-4 members): Prepare a budget scenario with computations and a brief recommendation. Choose ONE scenario below.

Scenario A: Student Travel Budget

Plan a 3-day educational trip for 5 students. Budget: ₱15,000 total. Include: transport, food, accommodation, activities, emergency fund. Show all computations with discounts and taxes.

Scenario B: Small Business Start-Up

You're starting a food cart business. Capital: ₱20,000. Plan: cost of supplies, markup pricing, projected daily revenue, monthly expenses, break-even analysis. When will you recover your capital?

Scenario C: Personal Finance Plan

A fresh graduate earns ₱18,000/month. Create a full budget with savings plan. Compare: saving ₱3,000/month in a bank (2% compound) vs. ₱3,000/month in a coop (5% simple). Project for 5 years.

Scenario D: Event Budget

Plan a class party for 40 people. Budget: ₱8,000. Include: food (with per-head computation), venue, decorations, prizes. Show unit pricing, totals, and where you applied discounts/negotiations.
