Deep Dive

Arithmetic Sequence Formula

aₙ = a₁ + (n − 1)d
SymbolMeaning
aₙThe term you want to find (nth term)
a₁First term of the sequence
nPosition number (which term)
dCommon difference (what you add)
Example: Find the 50th term

Sequence: 3, 8, 13, 18, 23, ...
a₁ = 3, d = 5, n = 50
a₅₀ = 3 + (50−1)(5)
a₅₀ = 3 + 245 = 248

Practice

Solve These (Arithmetic)

Problem 1

Find the 30th term of: 4, 11, 18, 25, ...

a₁=4, d=7, n=30
a₃₀ = 4 + (29)(7) = 4 + 203 = 207

Problem 2

A worker earns ₱500/day with ₱25 daily raise. What's Day 15 salary?

a₁=500, d=25, n=15
a₁₅ = 500 + (14)(25) = 500 + 350 = ₱850

Problem 3

Inventory starts at 500 items, depletes 12/day. Items on Day 20?

a₁=500, d=−12, n=20
a₂₀ = 500 + (19)(−12) = 500 − 228 = 272 items

Problem 4

What term of 2, 9, 16, 23... equals 156?

156 = 2 + (n−1)(7)
154 = 7(n−1)
n−1 = 22
n = 23 (23rd term)

Deep Dive

Geometric Sequence Formula

aₙ = a₁ × r⁽ⁿ⁻¹⁾
SymbolMeaning
aₙThe nth term
a₁First term
rCommon ratio (what you multiply by)
nPosition number
Example: Find the 8th term

Sequence: 3, 6, 12, 24, ...
a₁ = 3, r = 2, n = 8
a₈ = 3 × 2⁷
a₈ = 3 × 128 = 384

Practice

Solve These (Geometric)

Problem 1

Find the 6th term: 5, 15, 45, 135, ...

a₁=5, r=3, n=6
a₆ = 5 × 3⁵ = 5 × 243 = 1,215

Problem 2

Bacteria doubles hourly from 100. Count after 10 hours?

a₁=100, r=2, n=11 (hour 0 to 10)
a₁₁ = 100 × 2¹⁰ = 100 × 1024 = 102,400

Problem 3

₱10,000 depreciates 20%/year. Value after 5 years?

a₁=10000, r=0.8, n=6
a₆ = 10000 × 0.8⁵ = 10000 × 0.328 ≈ ₱3,277

Problem 4

Social media post shared by 2 people each: how many see it by Round 12?

a₁=1, r=2, n=12
a₁₂ = 1 × 2¹¹ = 2,048 people

The Golden Ratio

φ (Phi) = 1.618...

When you divide any Fibonacci number by the one before it, you get closer and closer to 1.618... (the golden ratio).

Applied Mathematics

Golden Ratio in Design & Art

🏛️ Architecture

Parthenon, pyramids, modern buildings use φ proportions for aesthetic appeal

🎨 Art & Photography

Rule of thirds (approximates golden ratio) for balanced compositions

📱 UI/UX Design

Apple, Twitter, Google logos use golden ratio proportions

📐 Typography

Font sizes often scaled by φ: 10, 16, 26, 42...

Investment Comparison

Option A: Fixed Deposit

₱1,000/month for 12 months
Pattern: ₱1K, ₱2K, ₱3K... ₱12K
Arithmetic (d=1000)
Total: ₱12,000

Option B: 5% Monthly Growth

Start ₱1,000, grows 5%/month
Pattern: ₱1K, ₱1.05K, ₱1.1K...
Geometric (r=1.05)
Month 12: ₱1,000 × 1.05¹¹ = ₱1,710

Key Insight for Future Accountants:

Understanding these patterns is essential for computing interest, analyzing investments, and advising clients on financial decisions.

Compound interest = geometric sequence
Regular deposits = arithmetic sequence

Wrap-Up

Today's Takeaways

  • Arithmetic: aₙ = a₁ + (n−1)d (linear growth)
  • Geometric: aₙ = a₁ × r⁽ⁿ⁻¹⁾ (exponential growth)
  • Fibonacci: Fₙ = Fₙ₋₁ + Fₙ₋₂ (add previous two terms)
  • Golden ratio φ ≈ 1.618 connects Fibonacci to nature & design
  • These patterns apply to savings, investments, natural growth, and more
